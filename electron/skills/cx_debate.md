# Policy / CX Debate Guide

## Overview
Two-on-two. Aff defends a plan under a year-long resolution. Neg opposes. Same resolution all season. Aff has burden of proof — if tied, Neg wins via presumption (status quo).

## Speech Order & Times

| Speech | HS Time | College Time | Notes |
|--------|---------|--------------|-------|
| 1AC | 8 min | 9 min | Pre-scripted; Aff opens |
| CX | 3 min | 3 min | Neg questions Aff |
| 1NC | 8 min | 9 min | All major Neg arguments |
| CX | 3 min | 3 min | Aff questions Neg |
| 2AC | 8 min | 9 min | Must answer everything |
| CX | 3 min | 3 min | Neg questions Aff |
| 2NC | 8 min | 9 min | Develops chosen Neg args |
| CX | 3 min | 3 min | Aff questions Neg |
| 1NR | 5 min | 6 min | Covers what 2NC didn't |
| 1AR | 5 min | 6 min | Must answer entire neg block — hardest speech |
| 2NR | 5 min | 6 min | Collapse to 1–2 args; frame the round |
| 2AR | 5 min | 6 min | Final speech; answer 2NR directly |

**Negative block:** 2NC + 1NR = 13 consecutive minutes (HS) or 15 (college) of Neg speaking time.
**Prep time:** 8 min/team (HS), 10 min/team (college). Used between speeches at the team's discretion.

## Aff Burdens (Stock Issues)
- **Inherency** — the problem exists and the status quo is not fixing it
- **Harms / Advantages** — significant negative consequences in the SQ that the plan addresses
- **Solvency** — the plan actually fixes or reduces those harms
- **Topicality** — the plan falls within the bounds of the resolution

## Neg Toolkit

### Disadvantages (DAs)
Plan causes harmful consequences that outweigh the advantages.
- **Uniqueness** — the harm doesn't exist in the status quo
- **Link** — the plan triggers the harm
- **Impact** — the harm itself (with magnitude, probability, timeframe)
- To beat a DA: turn it, no-link it, outweigh on impact calculus, or take out uniqueness

### Counterplans (CPs)
Alternative policy that solves the problem without the Aff's plan.
- Must be **competitive**: mutually exclusive (can't do both) or net better than the permutation (doing both)
- **Permutation test**: Aff argues "perm — do both" to show the CP isn't truly competitive
- Common CP types: consult CPs, process CPs, agent CPs, international fiat CPs

### Kritiks (Ks)
The Aff holds a flawed assumption or mindset that should be rejected independent of the plan's policy merits.
- **Link** — how the Aff's discourse/assumptions trigger the K
- **Impact** — the harm from those assumptions
- **Alternative** — what the Neg advocates instead
- Common Ks: capitalism, biopower, security, anthropocentrism, militarism, racism, ableism

### Topicality (T)
The Aff's plan does not fall within the bounds of the resolution → automatic reject regardless of plan merits.
- **Violation** — what the Aff did wrong
- **Standards** — why your interpretation is better (limits, ground, predictability)
- **Voting issue** — why T should be a voter (jurisdiction, fairness, education)

### Theory
Procedural fairness/education objections to how the debate is being conducted.
- Examples: conditionality bad (Neg can't run contradictory CPs and Ks), multiple worlds, PICS bad
- Components: interpretation, violation, standards, voting issue

## Evidence (Cards)
- **Tag** — 1–2 sentence summary of the argument
- **Citation** — author, publication, date (read aloud in round)
- **Body** — excerpt with key lines underlined/highlighted (read at speed)
- Sources: academic papers, government reports, news organizations, think tanks
- **Spreading** — extremely fast delivery to fit more arguments. National circuit standard. Know your judge.

## Flowing
Column-based note system, one column per speech, arguments tracked horizontally across the round. Each off-case argument (DA, CP, K, T) gets its own separate flow sheet. Use shorthand and symbols.

## Judging Paradigms
- **Policymaker** — who has the better policy outcome
- **Stock Issues** — Aff must win all 4 traditional burdens
- **Tabula Rasa** — judge evaluates whatever framework the debaters establish
- **Tech over Truth** — in-round argumentation > real-world accuracy
- **Truth over Tech** — common sense > technical wins

Always ask the judge's paradigm before the round.

## Speaker Points
Typically 26–29 scale. High 28s–29s for exceptional performance.

## Organizations
- **HS**: NSDA (primary), TOC (Tournament of Champions at University of Kentucky — requires qualifying bids at elite invitationals)
- **College**: NDT (National Debate Tournament) + CEDA (Cross Examination Debate Association) — merged topics 1996. Also ADA (American Debate Association).
- College constructives: 9 min. Rebuttals: 6 min. Prep: 10 min/team.
- HS constructives: 8 min. Rebuttals: 5 min. Prep: 8 min/team.

## HS vs College Differences
College topics are broader and more conceptually complex, requiring squad-wide research collaboration. Critical and performative approaches (non-traditional Affs, performance Ks) are significantly more prevalent in college. Kritiks, theory debates, and philosophical argumentation are more advanced. College debaters frequently coach and judge HS programs.
